Decoding Intentions to Migrate

Central America's Northern Triangle

Presented by Surbhi Agrawal, Deni Lopez, Sarah Jeong & Maria Daniela Castillo on December, 2021

BACKGROUND

The trends in the data indicate a strong correlation between intention to migrate and a person being young, male, dissatisfied with their living area, and who consider that migration abroad will bring good consequences to families.


The data showed moderate (but still statistically significant) correlations with respondents who report insufficient household income to purchase food, subject to one or more types of insecurity, and with households that have experienced a total loss of income due to the pandemic.


The main drivers for these trends, as surveyed by the Congressional Research Service, include difficult socioeconomic and security conditions - which are exacerbated by natural disasters and poor governance.

The impact of climate change on migration have been underestimated in some areas

RELATED FACTORS

The working-age populations of Northern Triangle countries are young, with 45% of Guatemalans, 42% of Hondurans and 36% of Salvadorans under the age of 20.


Central America has become drier and hotter in recent decades, with prolonged droughts since 2014. Crop loss and declining employment in the coffee sector have made especially rural families vulnerable to unemployment and hunger.
